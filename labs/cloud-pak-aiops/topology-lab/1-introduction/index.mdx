Coined by Gartner, AIOps—Artificial Intelligence for Operations—is the application of artificial intelligence (AI) capabilities, such as natural language processing and machine learning models, to automate and streamline operational workflows. In telecommunications environments, AIOps applies these capabilities to network operations, helping operators manage large-scale and complex infrastructures that include IP networks, optical transport systems, and service delivery platforms.
IBM Cloud Pak for AIOps provides an AIOps platform that uses advanced and explainable AI to analyze operational data coming from network monitoring, service management, and operational support systems (OSS). This allows telecom operators to confidently assess, diagnose, and resolve incidents that affect network infrastructure and customer-facing services.
Telecommunications networks are highly distributed and composed of multiple technology layers such as IP/MPLS routers, optical transport systems, and fiber infrastructure. Maintaining high service availability across this complex environment is critical. Cloud Pak for AIOps helps reduce operational complexity by correlating events, topology information, and operational insights from across the network, enabling operations teams to detect problems faster and identify the root cause of incidents.
By integrating multiple operational systems—such as network monitoring tools, event management systems, and automation platforms—into a unified and intelligent platform, AIOps enables Network Operations Center (NOC) teams to respond more quickly and even proactively prevent service degradation or outages. The platform provides end-to-end visibility across network infrastructure and services, helping operators maintain reliable connectivity and deliver high-quality services to customers.
Welcome to the Cloud Pak for AIOps Topology Lab for Telecommunications. In this lab, you will go through several exercises designed to help you learn how to ingest, visualize, and manage network topology and service dependencies using Cloud Pak for AIOps.
In telecommunications environments, topology data represents the relationships between network elements such as routers, optical transport devices, fiber links, and the services that depend on them. This information enables network operations teams to quickly understand the scope of an incident, identify impacted services, and determine the root cause of issues affecting network connectivity and customer services.

### Lab Content

In this Lab, we will focus on a subset of the main features available in
**Resource Management** such as:

- how to collect topology data
- how to use rules to merge topology elements
- how to use templates to gather resources into logical groups
- how to define applications from groups and business criticality
